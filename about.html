Our Story

About Rainy Mood

Pure rain sounds for sleep, focus, and meditation — no ads, no signups, no algorithms. Just the storm.

Why Rainy Mood Exists

Most rain-sound apps and YouTube videos interrupt the one thing you opened them for: quiet. Mid-roll ads jolt you awake. Autoplay surprises pull you out of focus. Looping audio glitches give the brain something to fixate on. Rainy Mood was built to solve all of that.

We generate rain procedurally in real time using the Web Audio API — every raindrop, wind gust, and thunderclap is synthesized live, so the loop never repeats and your ears never lock onto a pattern. The result is rain that feels real, plays forever, and stays out of your way.

Who Uses Rainy Mood

  • People with insomnia who need continuous noise masking through the night.
  • Students and remote workers who use rain as their focus background.
  • Parents who play rain sounds to soothe babies and toddlers.
  • Meditators, yoga teachers, massage therapists, and breathwork practitioners.
  • Anyone with tinnitus, ADHD, or anxiety who finds steady rain calming.
  • Travelers in hotels and airports who want to mask unfamiliar noise.

How It's Built

Rainy Mood runs entirely in your browser. Audio is synthesized client-side using the Web Audio API — there's no streaming server, no media buffer, no bandwidth cost. That's why it works offline once loaded, never repeats, and uses a fraction of the data of a 10-hour video.

The visual rain on the canvas is rendered in real time, too — every droplet is generated by the same physics engine driving the audio.

The Team

Rainy Mood is built by a small, independent team of designers, developers, and audio engineers who care deeply about sleep, focus, and the small joy of listening to rain on a window. We have no investors, no shareholders, and no growth-at-all-costs roadmap. Just rain.
